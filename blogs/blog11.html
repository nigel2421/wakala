Before Purchasing Land in Kenya, Do Your Research

Start on the right foot

A crucial step that any potential buyer in Kenya must take seriously is doing due diligence before purchasing property. In Kenya, buying land or property is a big investment that might put you at serious danger if you don't do the necessary legal research. These hazards can include dealing with dishonest vendors, inheriting unpaid land rates, or breaking zoning laws.

These are the main steps: –

1. Conduct a Search in the Official Land Registry

Conducting a formal search at the Land Registry is the first and most important step. You can use this search to confirm who owns the property and identify any encumbrances, such as mortgages, caveats, or outstanding disputes.

2. Check the Identity and Legal Status of the Seller

Check that the seller is the rightful owner of the property and has the authority to sell it. This entails verifying the seller's legal status and examining their identity documents. A firm must preserve the validity of the business, its directors, and its shareholders if it sells the property.

3. Perform a Physical Examination (Site Visit)

To confirm the property's condition, borders, and any existing structures, a physical investigation is required. Hire a certified surveyor to verify that the boundaries match those on official land maps and to look for any structural problems.

4. Check Land Use and Zoning Regulations

Confirm that the asset conforms with land use and zoning laws. This entails confirming with the local county government that the property is zoned for the intended purpose and that there are no upcoming limitations or infractions for the planned developments.

5. Examination of other documents, including the Sale Agreement.

Review all pertinent legal documents, such as contracts, agreements, licenses, and permits related to the property, with an experienced real estate lawyer.

6. Historical ownership and transfer approvals or consent.

To learn about the property's past ownership and other underlying encumbrances that the official search was unable to identify, get a copy of the green card from the relevant land registry. Additionally, obtain a land rent clearance certificate from the Ministry of Lands and land rates from the relevant county government. To sell the Land Control Board (LCB) approval for the agricultural land, make sure you have your spouse's consent.

7. Sale Agreement Execution

You can sign the selling agreement if you are happy with its conditions. The contract is executed when the buyer and seller sign it in front of their witnesses. After the transaction is completed, the buyer deposits 10% of the purchase price into an escrow account to provide protection in the event of any problems.

8. Finalization and Transfer of Title

You can move forward with the sale and title transfer once all due diligence procedures have been finished. This entails paying stamp duty, which is 2% for agricultural land and 4% for municipal/urban land, depositing all necessary paperwork with the land registrar to transfer ownership to the buyer, issuing a new title deed in the buyer's favor, and then finishing the payment of the remaining purchase price.

Conclusion

The foundation of secure off-plan real estate investments in Kenya is due diligence. Never make a payment commitment without first confirming ownership, approvals, and the legitimacy of the developer. Before you sign on the dotted line, a knowledgeable attorney makes sure that every box is checked.
